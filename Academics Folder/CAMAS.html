College of Arts, Management and Social Sciences(CAMAS)

About CAMAS

The subject matter of Natural and Applied Sciences revolves around understanding the principles governing the natural world and applying scientific knowledge to solve real-world problems. It encompasses the study of life sciences, physical sciences, computational sciences, and their technological applications. The advancement of these disciplines is essential for innovation, economic growth, and improving the quality of human life. At Chrisland University, CONAS is committed to equipping students with the scientific knowledge and practical skills needed to address contemporary challenges in health, technology, and environmental sustainability. Through a blend of theoretical instruction, laboratory research, and hands-on applications, the college fosters critical thinking, innovation, and problem-solving abilities among students.

Dean Of CONAS

Prof. Raphael Mordi

The College of Natural and Applied Sciences is led by Professor Raphael Chinyelum Mordi, an accomplished academic with extensive experience in scientific research and education. Under his guidance, the college offers diverse programs, including Computer Science, Software Engineering, Physics with Electronics, Microbiology, Biochemistry, Cybersecurity, Industrial Chemistry, Statistics, Biotechnology, and Industrial Mathematics. Professor Mordi's leadership ensures that students receive a comprehensive education that combines theoretical knowledge with practical application.
